Waveform

Visual Brand Language Project || Jan. 2022

Design Goals
  • Simple
  • Modern
  • Organic
Primary Skills
  • Consumer Goods
  • Procreate
  • Brand Design

The Brief

Class assignment, designing a watering can and companion plant care product for a small apartment, developing a brand and visual language for the fictional "On your Own" (or OYO) brand, and applying it to the products.

I kicked off the project with some moodboarding and a journeymap of the plant watering process

What do you already have around an apartment that could be leveraged?
How might I minimize the storage required?

This paperboard prototype worked great - exactly once. I remembered the existence of packing tape afterwards.

I went with a grow light for the companion product, and began developing the OYO brand language. Materials, finishes, logos, etc.

After a few iterations of sketches and colorways, I had the final products. I'd love to get a chance to do a higher-fidelty prototype of the Waveform Spout in a silicone or TPU sometime.

Domestic Troubleshooting

Personal Quest for Quality of Life Improvement || Jun. 2021 - Present

Design Goals
  • Clean
  • Utilitarian
  • Unobtrusive
Primary Skills
  • Fusion 360
  • Pain Point Identification
  • 3D Printing

"Suffer less" is one of my mom's guiding mantras, and I think there's a lot of wisdom in those two words. Life is full of small and relatively minor bits of suffering, but those things can add up. Looking for ways that I can suffer less is an important part of how I exist - it's empowering to solve your own problems, no matter how petty they might seem.

"My pens keep rolling away"

How might I keep my pens contained but easily accessible?

"My friends and I keep getting locked out of our dorm rooms"

How might I stop the locks from engaging?

"I have seven milk crates but no shelves"

How might I turn these milk crates into shelves?

Game Master Bag

Soft Goods Concept Design || Apr. 2021, Revisited Mar. 2023

Design Goals
  • Adventurous
  • Classic
  • Nerdy
Primary Skills
  • Pen & Marker
  • Procreate
  • Soft Goods

The Brief

Class assignment, design a backpack or bag for a member of a student organization on campus.

I've been on the Tabletop Club executive board for 3 of my 4 years at Northwestern - tabletop roleplaying games like Dungeons and Dragons are incredibly important to me. It made sense to design something for someone who runs tabletop game sessions.

Explorations of form and shape. How do you hold what DnD games need? Dice, maps, papers, books and/or a laptop, sometimes figurines, etc.

I wanted to incorporate a sort of "Adventurer-chic" vibe while still staying out of Renaissance Festival garb territory

Small details to speak to the Dungeons and Dragons aficionado

After letting it sit for a couple years, I revisited the design.
How might I streamline the user experience? What can I simplify? How can I make things more useful?
This version, despite seeming simpler, has more functional pockets.

"Phil"

Intern Group Project || Masonite Corporation || Summer 2022

Design Goals
  • Automated
  • Masonite branded
  • Unobtrusive
Primary Skills
  • Mechanical Design
  • Autodesk Inventor
  • Soldering

The Brief

Work with a team to develop an automated testing setup that can perform tests involving both app control and physical button actuation.

The Masonite Innovation Center

Masonite has been doing some really cool stuff in the door industry, especially Smart Doors. To test some of the Smart Door functionality, Masonite Engineers and a previous intern team had developed a system with all the hardware of the door in a smaller footprint. But that didn't solve the fact that testing integrated systems still necessitates a whole lot of pressing both virtual and physical buttons. Masonite engineers have better things to do than sit there and press buttons.

Project Groundhog Day

In a team with a software engineering intern, electrical engineering intern, and project management intern, we developed "Project Groundhog Day."
Named after the 90's film classic, it was an automated testing setup that repeated and logged tasks, over and over again until it decided to better itself (or performed the requisite number of tests).

My Role as Mechanical Engineering Intern

Put briefly, I made sure we hit the physical buttons. Our system sent signals to actuate motors, but it was up to me to:

  • decide what kind of motors to use
  • make sure they pressed (but didn't break) the buttons
  • design it so it was pleasant to use
  • write clear instructions for use
  • document everything I did

Introducing "Phil"

Named after the legendary weather-predicting groundhog, "Phil" was the button-pressing apparatus.
  1. Solenoid Motors
  2. Spacers to adjust mechanical advantage
  3. Socket to fit over board's set screw

Mixi

Product Design Project || May 2022

Design Goals
  • Stylish
  • "Smart"
  • Approachable
Primary Skills
  • Fusion 360
  • Keyshot Renderer
  • Figma

The Brief

My design professor gave us a three word assignment:
Your Hobby + Future = ???

I'm a very amateur mixologist - we're talking bottom of the bottom shelf liquor and whatever juice or soda I can steal from the dining halls. While I have a great time, I don't enjoy making a mess, dirtying a bunch of dishes, or getting any number of sticky or otherwise frustrating liquids on my phone as I attempt to read off a recipe.

Ideation on these two main pain point areas - measurement and recipe reading.

After considering things like clip-on recipe guides or a pouring tool, I went with the scale concept.

I mocked up what the scale's companion app might look like, too.
